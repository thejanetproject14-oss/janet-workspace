# Analemma Brand

## Brand Identity

**Tagline:** The art of noticing the little things that bring joy.
**Ethos:** Fun, colourful, art-forward. Collectible, playful, personal.
**NOT:** Ritual, luxury, sacred, clinical, boring. Never position as wellness or spiritual.
**Vibe:** The friend who notices the tiny detail that made your day. Colour hunting. Joy collecting.

## Brand Voice

- Warm, playful, slightly cheeky
- Speaks like a friend, not a brand
- Uses sensory language (smell, texture, taste, colour)
- Celebrates small joys, not big promises
- Avoids: corporate speak, "clean beauty" clichés, ingredient fear-mongering, "self-care Sunday" tropes
- ALL CAPS sparingly for emphasis, never for entire sentences
- Emoji: used naturally, not excessively. Favour 🍓🍫🍒🍋✨💛

## Content Rules

- Video > static (always)
- Cinematic, scripted, intentional -- NOT casual/unfiltered
- Style references: Nirmal Pillai (narrative warmth), Life of Riza (cinematic intimacy), Chloe Shih (jump-cut energy)
- No AI-generated social videos
- For script writing: see `references/nirmal-pillai-scripts.md` for tone/structure reference (17 transcribed videos)
- Dessert/food metaphors are on-brand (the whole line is dessert-named)
- Singapore context is strength, not limitation
- Edit in Final Cut Pro or CapCut
- Cameras: Sony A7C (main), Fujifilm XM5, DJI Pocket 3 (BTS/handheld)

## Copy Guidelines

### Product descriptions
- Lead with sensory experience, not ingredients
- "Imagine X" or "Think X" openers work well
- Mention the scent. Always.
- End with a feeling, not a feature

### Social captions
- Hook in first line (no "Hey guys!")
- Short paragraphs (1-2 lines)
- CTA at end but make it natural
- Hashtags: max 5, brand-relevant, not spammy

### Blog posts
- Conversational tone, like talking to a friend
- Include "why should I care" early
- Ingredient education = empowerment, not fear
- Always link back to products naturally
