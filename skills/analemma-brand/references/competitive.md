# Competitive Landscape

## Direct Competitors (SEA beauty, indie, ingredients-first)

### Love Bonito → Moom Health
- LB founded 2010, first store 2017, Series C 2022 (~$50M). Revenue $88M in 2023.
- Moom Health: LB-invested, founded 2021, ~22 employees, pre-Series A.
- Moom is in the same phase Analemma is entering now.
- Key insight: LB took 7+ years before serious VC money.

### Key takeaway
Analemma is pre-revenue, pre-traction. The comparison isn't "how do we compete with LB" -- it's "LB's trajectory proves SEA founder-led brands can reach scale."

## Category Positioning

Nobody occupies: **Ayurvedic philosophy × K-beauty formulation × SEA market.**

| Player | What they own | What they miss |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| K-beauty brands (Laneige, Innisfree) | Formulation science, texture | No Ayurvedic angle, no SEA founder story |
| Ayurvedic brands (Forest Essentials, Kama) | Ingredient philosophy | Too clinical/ritual, weak formulation, India-focused |
| SEA indie beauty | Local market access | No clear category, no dual-philosophy positioning |
| Analemma | Both + fun/art/joy angle | Needs proof: revenue, retention, community |

## Moat Potential
- Brand (hardest to copy -- personality, community, founder story)
- Formulation IP (unique combinations at scale)
- Category ownership (first to claim the intersection)
- Community/content engine (organic, defensible)

## Anti-positioning
Analemma is NOT:
- Clean beauty (don't lead with "free from" language)
- Luxury beauty (accessible, joyful, not exclusive)
- Wellness brand (fun, not functional)
- K-beauty clone (uses K-beauty science, not aesthetic)
- Ayurvedic brand (uses philosophy, not packaging)
