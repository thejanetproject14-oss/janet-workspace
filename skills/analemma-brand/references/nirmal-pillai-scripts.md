## 1. 5PM Feels Like...
*A personal musing on the magic of 5pm*

A lot of things. It's my beautiful 5pm. Feels like a lot of things. It's my favorite time of the day.

5pm feels like when you're on top of a Ferris wheel right before it's about to come down. It's what I think probably retirement feels like.

5pm feels like crossing a bridge. You know the ones where you stop your car, take a photo of you and the dried up river in the back. Those ones.

5pm feels like autumn. Or the idea of autumn. There's no autumn in Bangalore. Sorry.

5pm feels like when you pull the accelerator on a newly rented two-wheeler in Goa.

5pm feels like chai. Like ginger masala chai. With full fat, full dairy milk and some water — but less water.

5pm feels like the word *bas*. It's done. You did. Doing has happened. Now time for *bas*. The end of doing.

Maybe I should end this video too before 6pm comes and the pressure to enjoy your evening sets in.

## 8. Magical Alleys — The Street Entrepreneurs of India
*On the informal economy and the soul of Indian streets*

India is a country full of entrepreneurs. And no — I don't mean startups. Or big tech. Or people sitting in big, shiny glass buildings.

What I mean is: entrepreneurs on the road. Entrepreneurs on the footpath. Entrepreneurs in between roads and footpaths. Even entrepreneurs on top of entrepreneurs. Entrepreneurs on carts. Cart installations.

Welcome to Tipa Sandra — one of the many magical alleys of India. It's like the Room of Requirement. Anything you want, inside here you get. You want footwear, makeup, jewellery, dal, cloths, vegetables, speakers, toilets — you get it.

What we don't see is that behind every Padma Medical — there is also a Padma. There is a story. There are actual people who bet it all on this. There are dreams, hopes, and risks.

This guy had to register as a business, get a GST certificate, pay GST every month, run balance sheets, book profits. Half of us don't even know this stuff. And I'm glad that they do.

Because I love these streets. I love the crowd. I love the bad fonts on even worse Photoshopped images. Because they give the city the most elusive of things: **life.**

*Magical alleys.*

## 11. Growing Up in Chennai in the 90s
*Ode to Madras*

Growing up in Chennai in the 90s was a vibe. You're playing watermark cricket in the summer heat. Cooling down with a Rasna packet. Saving up for your favourite snack. Wishing for a rain holiday at school. Weekends at the beach. When the only mall you knew was Spencer Plaza and the only bazaar you knew was Pondy Bazaar.

There are new things to see. Old experiences to relive. And slowly, it feels like home again.

Because you know in your heart — no matter where you go, this city will stay with you.

*Madras is forever.*

## 12. The Most Important Object & the Lost Passport
*On losing things you hid too well*

So you have this very important object. And this very important object deserves a very important place to keep it. A place that should be out of sight — but also the most top-secret, super-secure location. Right where nobody can find it.

And you were right. Nobody could ever find it.

Including me. It's a passport.

The process of realizing I've lost something starts from here. *But I think it's in another place.* I open it. It's not there.

The absence is confounding. I walk around in circles with no particular direction, in the ether of my own stupidity. I check everywhere I think it is. Finish the post of the house. Even the most improbable situations — why would you keep a passport in the fridge?

You don't find it. You do the same course again. Mindlessly opening drawers. Wafting through the manifestation of the mess inside your brain. Finding other things you thought you lost forever — my power cable, my birth certificate.

Until you give up forever. **Outsmarted by yourself.**

That is, until you ask for help from a force far greater than even your greatest engineering.

*[Your mother.]*

## 17. 3AM & the Purpose Pandemic
*On being in your early 20s and not knowing what you're doing*

It's 3am. You are in your early 20s and you're standing at the precipice of adult life. And on the other side are the friends you'll make along the way, anxiety, existentialism — and most importantly, purpose. Or the lack of it.

I feel like we're in a pandemic of dissatisfaction with our own lives. Everybody — me, my friends, people our age — seem to be in a crisis of not finding purpose. Of doing something, but not *the thing* that we're meant to be doing.

But what are we meant to be doing? We don't know.

Have you heard the sentence *"I don't know what I'm doing with my life"* — and it sounds familiar? Not only because you've heard it. But because you've said it. To yourself.

Why do we have this inane need to have a purpose to exist? I don't know the answer, and I'm trying to find out for myself. But I'm more and more suspicious that the purpose is hiding in plain sight. Impossibly — not overthinking.

But whatever it is — maybe I just made this because I think more of us are feeling this. And it helps to know that the crisis is not yours alone. But maybe all of us.
