### 1.2 The Thyroid Nutrient Stack (Evidence-Based)

These are the minerals your thyroid literally cannot function without. All vegetarian-friendly.

#### SELENIUM -- The #1 Thyroid Mineral
- **Why:** Required for T4 → T3 conversion. 2024 meta-analysis (869 patients): selenium supplementation significantly decreased TSH in subclinical hypothyroid patients not on medication (that's you).
- **Dose:** 200mcg/day (selenomethionine form, best absorbed)
- **Food sources:** 2 Brazil nuts/day = ~200mcg selenium. This is the single best hack. Keep a bag in the kitchen.
- **Timing:** With lunch (away from iron if taking iron supplement)
- **Duration:** Minimum 6 months. Studies show TSH improvement at 6-month mark.
- **Available SG:** Brazil nuts at FairPrice, Cold Storage, or Saastha. Supplement: Nature's Way or Blackmores at Guardian/Unity.

#### ZINC
- **Why:** Zinc deficiency impairs thyroid hormone synthesis. RCT showed zinc + selenium together improved T3 significantly in hypothyroid overweight women.
- **Dose:** 15-30mg/day (zinc picolinate or zinc bisglycinate -- best absorbed forms)
- **Food sources:** Pumpkin seeds (you're already eating these for seed cycling!), chickpeas, lentils, dairy (paneer, cheese), cashews
- **Timing:** With dinner. Take 2+ hours away from iron supplements.
- **Caution:** Don't exceed 40mg/day. Take with food to avoid nausea.

#### IODINE -- Handle With Care
- **Why:** Raw material for thyroid hormones. BUT excess iodine can worsen autoimmune thyroid conditions.
- **Do NOT supplement** until we know your Anti-TPO status from bloodwork.
- **Food sources (safe amounts):** Iodized salt (use it in cooking), dairy (milk, paneer, curd), seaweed (nori sheets -- 1-2 sheets/week, not daily)
- **No kelp supplements.** The iodine content is wildly variable and can be 10-100x what you need.

#### VITAMIN D
- **Why:** 2024 study: Vitamin D supplementation for 12 weeks significantly improved TSH in hypothyroid patients. Deficiency prevalence in Hashimoto's is 92% vs 63% in healthy controls.
- **Dose:** Get tested first. If deficient (<30 ng/mL): 4000-5000 IU/day for 8 weeks, then 2000 IU/day maintenance. If borderline: 2000 IU/day.
- **You have a Vitamin D Friday cron already** -- but weekly dosing is less effective than daily. Switch to daily.
- **SG reality:** Despite being tropical, indoor lifestyle = many Singaporeans are deficient. You're likely low.
- **Timing:** With a fat-containing meal (it's fat-soluble). Lunch or dinner.

#### MAGNESIUM
- **Why:** Supports T4 → T3 conversion, reduces cortisol, helps sleep, helps cramps (MC days!). You should already be on this.
- **Dose:** 400-500mg/day magnesium glycinate (best for sleep + absorption, doesn't cause diarrhea)
- **Timing:** Before bed. Helps sleep onset -- exactly what you need.
- **MC days:** Can increase to 500-600mg.

#### IRON (you're already supplementing)
- **Why:** Iron deficiency impairs thyroid peroxidase activity (the enzyme that makes thyroid hormones).
- **Timing rule:** Take iron 4 hours away from thyroid-supporting supplements (selenium, zinc). Take with vitamin C for absorption.
- **Never take with:** dairy, coffee, tea, calcium. These block absorption by 50-80%.
- **Your current 8pm iron cron is good** -- just make sure you're not having chai or milk within 2 hours of it.

## Phase 2: Targeted Supplementation (Weeks 4-12, After Bloodwork)

### 2.1 Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) -- The Ayurvedic Thyroid Hero

**Evidence:** Double-blind RCT (2018) in subclinical hypothyroid patients:
- 600mg/day ashwagandha root extract for 8 weeks
- TSH decreased by **12.5% at 4 weeks** and **17.4% at 8 weeks**
- T3 and T4 both improved significantly (p<0.001)
- No adverse effects

**This is legitimate.** Not wellness marketing -- an actual placebo-controlled trial published in the Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine.

- **Dose:** 600mg/day KSM-66 ashwagandha (full-spectrum root extract)
- **Timing:** Morning, with breakfast/first meal
- **Duration:** 8-12 weeks minimum
- **Available SG:** KSM-66 brand at iHerb (ships to SG in 5-7 days), or Himalaya Ashwagandha at Guardian
- **Caution:** If Anti-TPO is very high (autoimmune confirmed), discuss with endocrinologist first -- ashwagandha is an immune modulator

### 2.2 Myo-Inositol + Selenium Combo

**Evidence:** 2024 meta-analysis + 2022 Frontiers in Endocrinology study:
- Myo-inositol (600mg) + Selenium (83mcg) for 6 months
- Significant TSH reduction, improved thyroid autoimmunity markers
- Better results than selenium alone

**This is the combo that keeps showing up in the latest research.**

- **Dose:** 600mg myo-inositol + 83mcg selenium (or use your 200mcg selenium from Brazil nuts/supplement)
- **Timing:** Morning, empty stomach
- **Available SG:** iHerb -- Jarrow Formulas Inositol 750mg or Now Foods Inositol powder
- **Start after bloodwork** -- we want to see your baseline first

### 2.3 Revisit Thyroxine? (Decision Framework)

After bloodwork, here's the decision tree:

```
TSH 4.5-10 (mild subclinical):
  ├── Anti-TPO negative → Try 3-month supplement protocol first
  ├── Anti-TPO positive → Consider low-dose levothyroxine (25-50mcg) + supplements
  └── Symptoms improving → Continue supplements, retest at 3 months

TSH >10 (moderate-severe):
  └── Levothyroxine recommended regardless. Supplements as adjunct, not replacement.
```

**Current evidence (AAFP 2021, Cleveland Clinic):** For mild subclinical hypothyroidism (TSH 4.5-10) without symptoms, treatment is optional but recommended if:
- Positive Anti-TPO antibodies (progression risk to overt hypothyroidism)
- Elevated cholesterol (thyroid connection)
- Trying to conceive (critical for fetal development)
- Symptomatic (fatigue, weight gain, hair loss, constipation)

**You tick the symptoms box.** If bloodwork shows TSH >7 or Anti-TPO positive, I'd advocate for combining low-dose levothyroxine WITH the supplement protocol. Not either/or.

### 3.2 Movement (Gentle, Consistent)

- **No HIIT or intense cardio right now.** High-intensity exercise raises cortisol. Your cortisol is already elevated. Gentle movement only until cortisol normalizes.
- Swimming is excellent (if foot allows later)

### 3.3 Stress Management (Cortisol is the Silent Thyroid Killer)
- **Elevated TSH and elevated cortisol are directly correlated** (PMC study, 54 subjects). Your body confirms this.
- Cortisol suppresses TSH-receptor sensitivity. Even if you fix everything else, chronic stress will keep TSH high.
- Daily: 10 min pranayama or meditation (you have a meditation cron -- use it)
- Weekly: 1 "do nothing" block (no work, no screens, just exist)
- Monthly: something that brings genuine joy (not productive joy -- pure joy)

### 3.4 Malassezia/Dandruff Protocol (Fix the Symptom While Fixing the Root)
The allergic rhinitis → dandruff → Malassezia → hypothyroidism chain:
- **Nizoral (ketoconazole 2%) shampoo:** 2-3x/week for 4 weeks, then 1x/week maintenance
- **Wash hair before bed** (not morning). Malassezia feeds on sebum overnight. Clean scalp = less morning allergens on pillow.
- **Anti-dust mite mattress protector** (even though it's Malassezia, dust mites amplify the allergic response)
- **Silk pillowcase** (less friction, less flaking, less Malassezia transfer)
- As thyroid function improves → scalp sebum normalizes → Malassezia reduces → rhinitis improves. The cascade reverses.
